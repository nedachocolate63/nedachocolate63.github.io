About

I am a Ph.D. student in Industrial Engineering (SSIE) at Binghamton University. My background includes a B.S. in Computer Engineering and an M.S. in Socioeconomic Systems Engineering. My work combines mathematical optimization, simulation, and machine learning with biomedical data analysis. Recently, I have been building gene co-expression networks from RNA-seq data (e.g., PEAC RA cohort) and applying community detection methods (e.g., Louvain/Leiden) to identify biologically meaningful modules, followed by pathway enrichment and cross-disease comparisons between RA and SLE. My broader goal is to translate complex biomedical data into interpretable models that support clinical and policy decision-making.
